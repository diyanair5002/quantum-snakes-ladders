Quantum Snakes & Ladders

Visualising Grover's Algorithm

The Translation Gap: Traditional quantum computing is taught through abstract linear algebra, creating a steep barrier to entry. This project bridges that gap by reimagining a classical game as a physical probability landscape.

1. The Quantum Counter
Classically, you move one token at a time. Here, we use a 6-qubit register. Applying a Hadamard gate "shatters" this token into an equal superposition, experiencing all 64 squares simultaneously.
2. The Magic Library Analogy
Finding the winner classically takes 32 tries. Quantumly, we treat the board like a dark room. The Phase Oracle flips the mathematical sign of our target, tagging it without looking at it directly.
3. Constructive Interference
When we apply the Diffusion Operator, the "Ladders" act as hardware coupling. They steer the probability wave to cancel out in the wrong places (purple) and add up perfectly on the winner (yellow).
